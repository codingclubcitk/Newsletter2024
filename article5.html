India, UK launch joint tech security initiative

India and the UK have launched a Technology Security Initiative to strengthen telecommunications security. This initiative will cover critical areas like semiconductors, artificial intelligence, quantum computing, and health technology. UK Foreign Secretary David Lammy met with Indian leaders, including Prime Minister Narendra Modi and National Security Adviser Ajit Doval. The initiative will be coordinated by the NSAs of both countries. The Technology Security Initiative aims to enhance the India-UK strategic partnership. It will promote economic growth in both countries by expanding collaboration in critical and emerging technologies. Government, private sector, academia, and research institutions will be involved in this effort. During their meeting, Indian External Affairs Minister S. Jaishankar and Lammy reviewed the Roadmap 2030 for bilateral relations. They expressed satisfaction with the progress so far and agreed to renew the roadmap to increase cooperation in various areas, including trade, investment, defense, and green energy. India and the UK also noted significant progress in negotiating a free trade agreement (FTA). They hope to conclude it soon for mutual benefit. The two countries will also deepen defense and security cooperation in the Indo-Pacific region. The Technology Security Initiative aims to elevate the India-UK strategic partnership by focusing on critical and emerging technologies, reinforcing existing collaborations, and establishing new cooperation mechanisms.
